# Big Ideas — week ending 2026-05-22

Google I/O ate the AI week (May 19-20): **Gemini 3.5 Flash** claimed frontier-rival agentic-coding scores (Terminal-Bench 2.1 76.2, MCP Atlas 83.6) at ~4× the latency of larger frontier models, and **Gemini Omni** launched as a multimodal world-model series. The Omni launch fed a now-consensus thesis — *world models as the post-LLM frontier* — crystallized across Fortune (Fei-Fei Li, LeCun, NVIDIA), a Royal Society Phil Trans A special issue, and multiple commentary posts. Two more theses hit critical mass: Ben Thompson's **"two kinds of inference"** (answer vs. agentic compute bifurcating the silicon stack) and **"data-center community opposition as structural AI-capex risk"** (Gallup 70% oppose; 69 active moratoriums).

Hardware and quantum saw an industrial-policy bombshell on May 21 — IBM + U.S. Department of Commerce launched **Anderon**, the first US-purpose-built quantum wafer foundry in Albany NY, as part of a ~$2B CHIPS distribution to nine quantum vendors. **NVIDIA**'s Q1 FY27 (May 20) printed $81.6B revenue, confirmed Vera Rubin shipping Q3 2026, and tripled capital returns. In bio, Lilly's **TRIUMPH-1** Phase 3 (May 21) put retatrutide at 28.3% mean weight loss at 80 weeks — the first injectable to reach bariatric-surgery-class loss in a pivotal trial — while a Baker-lab *Nature* review declared core de novo protein design "close to solved" and Johns Hopkins reported sub-20-minute intraoperative BCI mapping with >1,000-electrode micro-ECoG. SpaceX scrubbed the first **Starship V3** flight (May 21, hydraulic pin), retargeting May 22. Waymo had a humbling counterpoint: all freeway robotaxi rides paused, plus four Sun Belt cities, on May 21.
