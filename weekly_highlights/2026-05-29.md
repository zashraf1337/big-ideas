# Big Ideas — week ending 2026-05-29

The story of the week is the consolidation of frontier AI at Anthropic: Claude Opus 4.8 shipped (69.2% on agentic coding, 84% computer-use), Anthropic disclosed a record $65B Series H at $965B post-money, and Karpathy announced he's joining the pretraining team to lead a "use Claude to accelerate pretraining" effort. Bio matched it: Lilly's PCSK9 base editor VERVE-102 cut LDL-C by up to 62% in a single dose (NEJM, May 25), and Lilly's triple-agonist retatrutide hit 28.3% weight loss at 80 weeks in Phase 3 TRIUMPH-1 — bariatric-surgery territory from a once-weekly injection.

Physical-world wins and losses both happened: SpaceX flew the first Starship V3 from Pad 2 on May 22; Blue Origin lost a New Glenn on the pad in a static-fire failure on May 28. IBM committed $10B over five years to deliver fault-tolerant Starling by 2029 (with the new Anderon foundry), Pasqal showed logical qubits outperform physical qubits on real differential equations on a neutral-atom processor, and PsiQuantum signed a $100M CHIPS LOI. Two clear field-level theses crystallized this week: world models as the next paradigm beyond LLMs (LeCun-group LeJEPA theory paper, Jim Fan's AI Ascent talk, Fortune feature) and "token-cost anxiety" — multiple independent voices (Microsoft, Vantage, Exponential View) hitting the same point that agentic-coding economics break the SaaS fixed-cost model.
