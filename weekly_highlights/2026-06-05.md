# Big Ideas — week ending 2026-06-05

Two conferences set the agenda this week. ADA 2026 turned into an obesity-drug showcase that broadened the field well beyond the GLP-1/GIP incretins: Boehringer's glucagon/GLP-1 dual agonist **survodutide** posted full SYNCHRONIZE-1 data (16.6% mean weight loss at 76 weeks), Innovent's **mazdutide** beat semaglutide head-to-head in DREAMS-3, and Roche surfaced **amylin analogs** (petrelintide) as a tolerability-differentiated class that can be co-formulated with incretins — a genuinely new mechanistic lane. Alongside, Moderna/Merck's **intismeran** melanoma vaccine reported strong 5-year survival at ASCO (49% lower recurrence, ~53% OS benefit). Meanwhile Computex 2026 and a wave of Chinese open-weight releases reframed the frontier narrative: **MiniMax M3** (1M-context sparse attention, 59% SWE-Bench Pro, open weights) and **NVIDIA Cosmos 3** (two-tower world model) both shipped, **Unitree** cleared a China STAR Market IPO and became NVIDIA's GR00T reference humanoid, and NVIDIA unveiled the **RTX Spark** client AI superchip.

On the physical-tech side, **Focused Energy** closed a $240M laser-fusion Series A (led by RWE), SpaceX's drone ship **OCISLY** logged its 200th booster catch, **Blue Origin** vowed New Glenn return-to-flight before year-end after the May 28 pad explosion, and **CATL** confirmed sodium-ion mass production for 2026. The week's clearest thesis-level trend is **"context/eval engineering as the new agent bottleneck"** — the argument, now repeated across The New Stack, Hugging Face, and SiliconANGLE, that frontier models are "good enough" and the binding constraint has shifted to feeding agents the right context and paying for evaluation. The standout new voice is **David Klindt** (Cold Spring Harbor), whose Lean-4-formalized proof (with LeCun and Balestriero) of *when* JEPA world models recover true latents went viral — a rare injection of formal rigor into the world-models debate.
